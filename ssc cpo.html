SSC CPO Selection Process 2025

The SSC CPO exam has four different phases that candidates need to clear in order to get selected for various posts in Delhi Police, CAPFs, CISFs, etc. Here are the different phases:

  • Tier 1 - Objective Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) Exam (Online)
  • PET/PST - Physical Endurance Test/Physical Standard Test
  • Tier 2 - English Comprehension Test (Online)
  • DME - Detailed Medical Examination

The SSC CPO Paper 2 will be conducted after the PET/PST Test for SSC CPO 2025 is completed.

Exam Overview:

  • Total Questions: 200 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ)
  • Total Marks: 200
  • Subject: English Language and Comprehension
  • Mode of Exam: Online
  • Negative Marking: 0.25 marks for each wrong answer

SSC CPO Medical Test Criteria

Vision Standards

  • Minimum Near Vision: N6 (better eye) and N9 (worse eye)
  • Minimum Distant Vision: 6/6 (better eye) and 6/9 (worse eye)

Physical Criteria

  • Must not have knock knee, flat foot, varicose vein, or squint in eyes

Other Requirements

  • Eye standards should be without visual correction of any kind, even by glasses.

SSC CPO Syllabus 2025

Overview of SSC CPO Exam

The SSC CPO exam is conducted in two phases: Paper 1 (Tier 1) and Paper 2 (Tier 2). The syllabus for each paper is different, and candidates should stay updated to prepare effectively for the 2025 exam.

SSC CPO Paper 1 Syllabus

In Paper 1, there are four sections that test different abilities:

  • Reasoning Ability: Logical thinking and analytical ability.
  • Quantitative Aptitude: Mathematical ability, including arithmetic and problem-solving skills.
  • English Language: Grammar, vocabulary, reading comprehension, and error correction.
  • General Awareness: Knowledge of current events, general knowledge, and basic facts about various subjects.

SSC CPO Paper 2 Syllabus

English Language and Comprehension

The English Language section in SSC CPO Paper 2 is designed to assess your comprehension and reading skills. It includes various sub-sections like error recognition, vocabulary, and grammar. Below is a breakdown of the syllabus:

Section Syllabus
Error Recognition Identify and correct errors in sentences.
Fill in the Blanks Choose appropriate words to fill the blanks in sentences.
Vocabulary Test your knowledge of word meanings and usage.
Spellings Identify correct spellings in words.
Grammar Focus on proper sentence structure, tense, and parts of speech.
Sentence Structure Test understanding of how to form grammatically correct sentences.
Synonyms Choose words with similar meanings to a given word.
Antonyms Select words with opposite meanings to a given word.
Sentence Completion Complete sentences with appropriate options to make them meaningful.
Idioms and Phrases Interpret idiomatic expressions and phrases correctly.
Comprehension Read passages and answer questions based on them.
